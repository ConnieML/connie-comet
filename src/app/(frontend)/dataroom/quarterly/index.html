Business Operations (Corp.) - Corporate Structure & Financing

Current State

Connie Digital is currently pre-revenue and debt-free. Initial prototype development was successfully bootstrapped, funded by a $150,000 grant and strategic incubation support from the American Society on Aging (ASA). This demonstrates early validation and prudent financial management.

Strategic Objective

To secure $365,000 in working capital by the end of Q3 2024. This capital is the critical catalyst required to transition from a proven prototype to a market-ready Minimum Viable Product (MVP) and fund the initial launch phases.

Analysis & Rationale

The requested capital falls within the typical Pre-Seed to Seed funding range for social impact tech startups, which often varies from $100,000 to over $1 million. Our approach is to raise precisely what is needed to achieve key milestones, maintaining lean operations and maximizing equity preservation. Funding will be sought from sources aligned with our dual-return model (financial and social).

  • Investor Profile: Our ideal partners are Impact Investors, Foundations with technology-for-good programs, and Angel Investors with a focus on social enterprise or Age-Tech. These investors understand the value of patient capital and the necessity of demonstrating measurable social outcomes alongside financial returns, a key differentiator for social impact ventures.
  • Use of Funds: The capital is not for speculative growth but for a targeted execution plan with a clear return on investment.

Technical Ops - MVP Development

Current State

A functional proof-of-concept prototype has been completed and successfully tested with co-design partners. This de-risks the technical approach and validates the core value proposition with the target user base.

Strategic Objective

Execute a focused MVP development sprint in August 2024 to build a secure, scalable, and market-ready platform. The MVP will embody a product-led growth (PLG) philosophy, designed for easy adoption and immediate value realization.

Analysis & Rationale

The nonprofit tech landscape is littered with complex, expensive software that requires dedicated IT staff. Our GTM strategy hinges on delivering a product that is the opposite: intuitive, self-service, and affordable.

As validated by case studies of nonprofit tech adoption, CBOs often struggle with "outdated systems" and a "lack of internal IT support." Our MVP must directly solve this by being deployable in minutes, not months.

The MVP feature set is strictly prioritized based on CBO feedback to solve their most acute pain points: unified communications, automated client updates, and simple reporting. This aligns with SaaS best practices where the initial product delivers a powerful solution to a narrow problem, creating a beachhead for future expansion.

Key Actions & Tactics

  1. Finalize Technical Roadmap: Lock the MVP feature set based on prototype feedback, prioritizing features that deliver the highest efficiency gains for CBOs.
  2. HIPAA Compliance Audit: Engage a third-party auditor to validate the architecture and ensure all data handling protocols meet stringent HIPAA requirements before the first line of MVP code is written.
  3. Execute Development Sprint: Dedicate engineering resources for the month of August to focused, agile development.
  4. Develop Onboarding Materials: Create step-by-step tutorials, a knowledge base, and in-app guides. A seamless onboarding experience is fundamental to product-led growth and is critical for users who may have lower digital literacy.

Metrics for Success

  • Primary KPI: On-time completion of the feature-complete MVP by August 31, 2024.
  • Secondary KPIs:
    • Successful third-party validation of HIPAA compliance.
    • Time-to-Value (TTV) for new users during testing is under 30 minutes.

Business Operations (Growth) - Audience & Pipeline Development

Current State

Our primary channel for audience and pipeline development is our strategic incubation with the American Society on Aging (ASA), granting trusted access to over 5,000 member organizations.

Strategic Objective

To systematically convert the ASA network into a qualified sales pipeline by implementing a multi-channel, content-driven engagement strategy. The goal is to build a waitlist of 50+ qualified CBOs by the end of Q1 2025.

Analysis & Rationale

This approach is a highly efficient form of Account-Based Marketing (ABM). Instead of expensive, broad-market campaigns, we are targeting a pre-vetted, high-affinity audience. Success requires moving beyond simple awareness and establishing Connie as a thought leader and indispensable partner.

Our marketing tactics will be rooted in inbound and content marketing, which is proven to be effective for B2B tech companies serving niche markets. We will create valuable content that addresses the core operational and mission-based challenges of CBO leaders.

Key Actions & Tactics

  1. Develop Foundational Content: Create white papers, case studies (from prototype partners), and blog posts focused on CBO efficiency, impact measurement, and digital transformation in the aging sector.
  2. Launch Targeted Webinar Series: In partnership with ASA, host webinars for members on topics like "Scaling Impact: How Technology Can Help You Serve 60% More Seniors" and "Beyond Spreadsheets: Reporting Your Impact to Funders."
  3. Engage on LinkedIn: Systematically identify and connect with decision-makers (Executive Directors, Program Managers) at key ASA member organizations, sharing relevant content and insights.
  4. ASA Conference Activation: Secure a speaking slot or workshop at the next ASA conference to conduct live demos and capture high-intent leads directly.

Metrics for Success

  • Primary KPI: 50+ qualified CBOs on the product waitlist by March 31, 2025.
  • Secondary KPIs:
    • Lead-to-Opportunity Conversion Rate from marketing activities.
    • Engagement metrics (webinar attendance, content downloads, social media engagement).

Business Operations (Growth) - Beta Program Launch

Current State

A plan is in place for a two-phased beta program following MVP completion.

Strategic Objective

To execute a Closed Beta in Q4 2024 to gather final, in-depth user feedback and refine the product, followed by a Paid Public Beta in Q1 2025 to validate the commercial model and generate initial revenue.

Analysis & Rationale

The beta program is our bridge from product development to commercialization. It serves three critical functions:

  1. Product Refinement: The Closed Beta with 3-5 trusted CBO co-design partners allows for final bug squashing and usability testing in a controlled environment.
  2. Commercial Validation: The Paid Public Beta is the ultimate test of market fit. Asking organizations to pay, even a discounted rate, validates that Connie is solving a problem valuable enough to allocate budget for.
  3. Social Proof Generation: The program is designed to create our first cohort of champions. Their success stories and testimonials will be the most powerful marketing assets for our full-scale launch.

Business Operations (Corp.) Legal, Compliance & Risk Management

Current State

The product architecture is designed for HIPAA compliance from the ground up, and all partner discussions are protected by NDAs.

Strategic Objective

To ensure Connie Digital operates with the highest standards of data security and regulatory compliance, establishing trust as a core pillar of the brand and a key competitive differentiator.

Analysis & Rationale

For our target market, trust is not a feature; it is the foundation. Handling the sensitive data of older adults and the operational data of CBOs carries immense responsibility. Proactive and transparent management of compliance and security is non-negotiable and provides a significant advantage over generic communication tools (e.g., social media, standard email) that cannot offer HIPAA assurances.

Key Actions & Tactics

  1. Formalize Policies: Develop and ratify official internal policies for Data Privacy, Security Incident Response, and HIPAA Compliance.
  2. Business Associate Agreements (BAA): Prepare a standardized BAA to be executed with all CBO customers, a legal requirement for handling Protected Health Information (PHI).
  3. Cybersecurity Insurance: Secure appropriate liability and cybersecurity insurance coverage prior to the Public Beta launch.
  4. Intellectual Property: Work with legal counsel to file for trademark protection for the "Connie" name and logo.

Metrics for Success

  • Primary KPI: Zero security incidents or data breaches.
  • Secondary KPIs:
    • All beta customers have executed a BAA prior to onboarding.
    • Trademark and insurance policies are in place by end of Q4 2024.
